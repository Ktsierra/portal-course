Intune Company Portal — Very Simple Guide

A gentle, step-by-step presentation for end users. Everything spelled out clearly.

Why use Company Portal? (Short and simple)
- It lets you get work apps easily.
- It keeps your computer and data safe.
- It helps IT push updates and fix problems faster.
Tip: Think of Company Portal like the "company's app store" for work stuff.
Step A — Find the Company Portal
1
Click the Windows Start button (the button at the bottom-left of the screen). If you are on Windows: it's a little window icon.
Example: Click the Start icon or press the Windows key on your keyboard.
2
In Start, type: Company Portal (or just type company).
You will see "Company Portal" or "Intune Company Portal" in the search results.
3
Click the app named Company Portal (icon looks like a suitcase/briefcase or a shield).
If you can't find it, look for the word "Intune" near its name.
Step B — Make it easy to open (Pin it)
1
Right-click the Company Portal in the Start search results.
If you have a touch screen, press and hold until the menu appears.
2
Choose one of these:
  1. Pin to taskbar — puts the app at the bottom of your screen so you can open it with one click.
  2. Pin to Start — places a tile in the Start menu for quick access.
If you do not see "Pin to taskbar", hover over "More" then choose "Pin to taskbar".
Step C — Open Company Portal and install an app
1
Open the Company Portal app (click the icon you pinned, or open from Start).
2
Look for a menu or a tab called Apps or Available apps. This is the place with apps your company allows you to install.
Sometimes there is a big button saying "Apps" — click it.
3
Find the app you want. You can:
  1. Scroll through the list.
  2. Or use the search box and type the app name.
4
Click the app to open its details page. Then click Install or Get.
The button might say "Install", "Get", or "Request" (if your company requires approval).
After clicking Install, wait — installation runs in the background. You can keep working. When it's done, the app may appear in your Start menu.
Step D — Read the app details (what you should check)
  1. Description: what the app does in short sentences.
  2. Publisher: the company who made the app (e.g., Microsoft, Adobe).
  3. Permissions: what the app needs to do (like access files). If it asks for a lot, ask IT.
  4. Install size and version: shows how big the app is and which version it is.
If you're unsure, read the description slowly. Ask a friendly IT person: "What does this app do?" — it's okay to ask.
If something doesn't work — quick help
- App not in list? Search again, then ask IT if you can't find it.
- Install button is "Request"? That means IT needs to approve — wait a little or contact IT.
- Installation failed? Try restarting your computer, then try again.
- Company Portal not installed? You might need to contact your IT help desk.
Keep calm. Most problems have simple fixes. Take a screenshot and send it to IT if you can't solve it.
What about privacy?
- The Company Portal can help protect company data on your device.
- IT can manage work apps and settings, but they do not read your personal files or messages.
If you have personal files, keep them in separate folders (like Documents) and do not mix with work resources.
Wrap-up — Quick checklist before you go
  1. Find Company Portal via Start (type "Company Portal").
  2. Pin it to the taskbar for easy one-click access.
  3. Open it, go to Apps, find the app, click Install.
  4. Read app details and check permissions if unsure.
  5. Contact IT if something needs approval or fails to install.
